Hubmetrix Release Notes version 1.1.2

These are the most recent notes for the latest release above.

Timeline Integration:

As of 1.1.0 we now have timeline integration! We will push new orders onto the customer's timeline with order date and order source. We will also update the timeline with order status information. Please be aware that due to the way that timeline updates are handled, the order status may end up appearing chronologically before the order in the timeline. This behavior may be fixed in the future if it becomes too much of an issue.

Order Updates:

Due to the way BigCommerce handles webhooks for orders, we will update HubSpot with data on order creation and order status updates. This means that if you edit an order with a new total or other information, we won’t send that data into HubSpot until the status changes. Changes will show up in HubSpot, for example, when you ship or otherwise change from the initial status. This prevents unnecessary data transfers. Remember though, that the initial order will always go straight to HubSpot, usually in less than 1 second. Here, we are simply referring to any subsequent changes to an order.

Customer Creation:

We will update HubSpot with new customer information when either a customer places an order, or when a customer is updated. We will not update the customer in HubSpot when the customer is initially created. This is to minimize duplicate traffic, and hopefully strikes a balance with how BigCommerce and HubSpot are used together. This may change in the future.

Customer Address Book Updates:

Currently due to the way that BigCommerce implements webhooks, we won’t update the customer in HubSpot immediately after updating only a customer’s address in BigCommerce. If you update a customer address book entry, and there is a subsequent change in order status, or a field in the main customer properties section, the customer will then be updated in HubSpot. This is a kind of “eventual consistency” approach that allows us to make sure customers are updated in HubSpot when it matters, but avoid sending excessive traffic to HubSpot.

Lifecycle Stages:

We will update your contact’s lifecyclestage property to one of the following—‘customer’ for those that have the property “all_time_total_revenue greater” set to something greater than 0, and ‘lead’ for anything other than that. NOTE: HubSpot’s lifecycle stage property is designed to move in a forward fashion only, unless explicitly set to blank first and then moved backward. We will not perform this operation. If your contact has a status greater than ‘lead’ and no revenue when we attempt to set the property to ‘lead’, the property change will not succeed (although the other settings will go through) and the contact will be left with whatever lifecyclestage they had before the operation.

Contact Sync Frequency:

Please note that contacting syncing will not happen all at once, or on a schedule. Hubmetrix is event driven, which means that contacts will sync when there is activity for that contact in BigCommerce. As mentioned above, there are some caveats, but basically this means that when a customer has a new order or a change to their customer details, they will then be synced with HubSpot.
